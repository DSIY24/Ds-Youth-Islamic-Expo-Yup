Competitions

Qirat (Qur'an Recitation) Competition

  1. Open to Islamic students only.
  2. Open to seniors, juniors and sub-juniors.
  3. Each age group can have a maximum of 2 boys and 2 girls.
  4. The Qirat Competition will consist of two rounds:
  5. The first round will be conducted in separate assigned classrooms, in which all participants will be given the opportunity to recite their assigned Surah (according to their age group, as specified in the table below).
  6. he second round will include a certain number of students who have qualified from the first round, in which the selected students will recite their assigned Surah in the auditorium, in front of all participating students.
  7. There will be 6 awards (across all age groups for boys and girls):
  8. 1st Place (Seniors - Boys)
  9. 1st Place (Seniors - Girls)
  10. 1st Place (Juniors - Boys)
  11. 1st Place (Juniors - Girls)
  12. 1st Place (Sub Juniors - Boys)
  13. 1st Place (Sub Juniors - Girls)
  14. The participants must recite the Surah from the beginning for a duration of 3 minutes.
  15. The time will be kept track of with assistance from a Core Team Member and volunteer. Please confirm with them regarding the same prior to the competition.
  16. While reciting, if a participant starts the recitation again (due to some error), then the time will not be recorded again.
  17. Participating students should only recite the surah mentioned in the following table (based on their age group).

Sustainable Art Competition​​

  1. Open to all students (Islamic and non-Islamic).
  2. Open to Sub-Juniors, Juniors and Seniors.
  3. Each age group can have a maximum of 2 boys and 2 girls.
  4. The Sustainable Art Competition consists of only one round.
  5. The art work should either have a reference of any ayah of the Holy Quran, a phrase/quotation from your respective divine book or a line/quote from your favourite author's book with reference to the theme of the event (i.e. ‘Sustainable Expressions’).
  6. There are three aspects (for the criteria) as mentioned in the rubric: Follows theme, creativity & presentation, where each criterion is 5 points, and therefore, a total score of 15 points.
  7. The competition is art in any form: painting, calligraphy, a 2D or 3D model.
  8. Students must participate in this sub-category individually and can only submit one art work.
  9. The students will be asked to talk about their art work for a duration of 3 minutes.​
  10. The time will be kept track of with assistance from a Core Team Member and volunteer. Please confirm with them regarding the same prior to the competition.
  11. While speaking, if a participant starts the speech again (due to some error), then the time will not be recorded again.
  12. It is up to the creativity of the participants to choose the size and dimensions of the paper/material on which they would do the art.
  13. All materials need to be recyclable and sustainable and brought from home.
  14. Students to get their model semi-created and assemble it in Dubai Scholars on the day of the competition (15th January 2023). The model must have an A4 size sheet indicating the category, student name and school name clearly.
  15. There will be 6 awards (across all age groups for boys and girls):
  16. 1st Place (Seniors - Boys)
  17. 1st Place (Seniors - Girls)
  18. 1st Place (Juniors - Boys)
  19. 1st Place (Juniors - Girls)
  20. 1st Place (Sub Juniors - Boys)
  21. 1st Place (Sub Juniors - Girls)

Elocution Competition

  1. Open to all students (Islamic and non-Islamic).
  2. Open to Seniors only.
  3. This specific competition can only have 2 boys and 2 girls from the Senior age group.
  4. There is only one round for the elocution competition, which will be treated as the final round. There will be 2 awards:
  5. 1st Place Winner (Girls)
  6. 1st Place Winner (Boys)
  7. The participating students should choose one of the following sub-topics as their topic for the Elocution Competition:
  8. Sustainability in a post-COVID world
  9. Innovation meets sustainability
  10. The power of gratitude
  11. Spiritual and social gratitude
  12. The time limit for the speech is 5 minutes.
  13. The time will be kept track of with assistance from a Core Team Member and volunteer. Please confirm with them regarding the same prior to the competition.
  14. The speech (except Quranic verses and Hadith) may not be plagiarised as the speech should reflect the speaker's understanding and research. Points will be deducted for plagiarism. ​
